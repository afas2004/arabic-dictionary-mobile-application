Reading the results

Most "earlier-tier" hits are wins, not regressions. Nine nominal derivatives expected to reach T7 fuzzy-root (مكتوب، مدارس، استخدام، كاتب، مكاتب، انكسار، مسجد، مستخدم) and four past-tense verbs expected to reach T2 (درس، وجد، مد، قرأ) are already stored as full lexicon entries. T1 catches them directly, skipping pattern analysis entirely — that's strictly better: faster, with higher confidence, and no guessing.

One known false early-hit: كتابي ("my book") lands on T3 with stripped clitic ك and resolves to تَابَ (to repent), not كِتَاب. The intended path is T5 stripping ي → كتاب. The cascade currently tries single-letter proclitics (T3) before enclitics (T5), so whenever both are applicable and the clipped front form is itself a valid conjugation, the noun loses. Fix options (for future work): (a) require T3 matches to land on base-verb/noun rows only, filtering out derived conjugations; or (b) reorder the cascade so T5 runs before T3. Both trade off latency vs. correctness in different ways.
